About

I am a faculty member at IT University of Copenhagen, Denmark, and I have been here since September 2021. Before this, I had the pleasure of serving as a faculty member in the Computer Science and Engineering department at the Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay, India from August 2010 to August 2021. In addition, I had a brief but fulfilling postdoctoral stint at the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research from August 2009 to July 2010. I completed my Ph.D. at the Institute of Mathematical Sciences, Chennai, India in 2009, where I was advised by Meena Mahajan.

Research

I am interested in several topics in theoretical computer science. My research mainly focuses on Complexity Theory. In the recent years, I have been spending most of my time thinking about problems in algebraic complexity theory.

Short bio

    Dr. Nutan Limaye is an Associate Professor in the Computer Science Department at IT University of Copenhagen, Denmark. Before this, she was an Associate Professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, IIT Bombay, India. She finished her Ph.D. at the Institute of Mathematical Sciences (IMSc) Chennai, India in 2009. She did a one-year postdoc stint at TIFR Mumbai, before joining IIT Bombay in 2010.

    Her research interests are in theoretical Computer Science. Specifically, she is interested in Algorithms and Complexity Theory. Her recent research has focussed on Algebraic Complexity Theory and Lower Bounds.

    She is an Associate Editor for ACM Transactions on Computation Theory (TOCT) and . She received the IRCC IIT Bombay Research Publication Award 2019 and the Best Paper award at FOCS 2021. She received the Departmental Award for Excellence in Teaching 2022 for her contributions to teaching activities at IIT Bombay. She has been on program committees of reputed computer science conferences such as STOC, FOCS, ITCS, and ICALP.

    She has been a part of the Association of Computing Machinery in many different capacities. Presently, she is the ACM-W Regional Activities Co-Chair. She was associated with ACM India as the vice-chair of the ACM-W from 2019 to 2021. She was also the treasurer for Indian Association for Research in Computing Science (IARCS), India from 2018 to 2020.
